TVA Nuclear Plant Safety Procedures Overview

FACILITIES
TVA operates three nuclear power facilities totaling 7 operating units:
- Browns Ferry Nuclear Plant (Athens, AL): Units 1, 2, 3 — 3,400 MW total
- Sequoyah Nuclear Plant (Soddy-Daisy, TN): Units 1, 2 — 2,400 MW total
- Watts Bar Nuclear Plant (Spring City, TN): Units 1, 2 — 2,300 MW total

All facilities hold current NRC operating licenses and operate under 10 CFR 50 regulations.

REACTOR OPERATING PARAMETERS
Primary Coolant System:
- Normal operating temperature: 540–560°F (282–293°C)
- Normal operating pressure: 2,235 psia
- Maximum design temperature: 620°F (327°C)
- Maximum design pressure: 2,500 psia

EMERGENCY CORE COOLING SYSTEMS (ECCS)
Three independent ECCS trains provide redundancy:
1. High-Pressure Injection System — activates on low reactor coolant pressure
2. Low-Pressure Injection System — provides long-term core cooling
3. Accumulator Injection — passive, nitrogen-pressurized backup

Testing frequency: Quarterly per NRC 10 CFR 50 Appendix A requirements
Last Browns Ferry ECCS test: January 2026 — all systems nominal

SAFETY CULTURE
TVA maintains a safety-conscious work environment (SCWE) per NRC requirements.
Employees are encouraged to raise nuclear safety concerns without fear of retaliation.
Safety concern reporting: 1-800-TVA-SAFE or safety@tva.gov

REGULATORY OVERSIGHT
- NRC Region II oversees all TVA nuclear facilities
- Annual NRC inspections conducted at each site
- Corrective Action Program (CAP) tracks all identified issues to resolution

EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS
- 10-mile Emergency Planning Zone (EPZ) established around each facility
- Emergency Response Organization (ERO) staffed 24/7
- Annual emergency drills conducted with state and local agencies

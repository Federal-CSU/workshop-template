TVA Grid Reliability Annual Report 2025 — Executive Summary

RELIABILITY PERFORMANCE
TVA's transmission system achieved 99.97% reliability in 2025, exceeding the NERC TPL-001 planning standard.
- SAIDI (System Average Interruption Duration Index): 68 minutes — improved 12% year-over-year
- SAIFI (System Average Interruption Frequency Index): 1.1 interruptions per customer
- Transmission line availability: 99.94%

GENERATION PORTFOLIO (as of December 2025)
Total installed capacity: 34,400 MW
- Nuclear: 7,500 MW (Browns Ferry, Sequoyah, Watts Bar) — 21.8%
- Natural Gas Combined Cycle: 9,200 MW — 26.7%
- Hydro: 4,900 MW (49 dams, Tennessee River system) — 14.2%
- Coal: 5,400 MW — 15.7%
- Renewable (solar + wind): 6,200 MW — 18% (up from 12% in 2024)
- Pumped Storage: 1,200 MW

RIVER MANAGEMENT
TVA manages 49 dams across the Tennessee River system serving:
- Flood control: prevented estimated $2.1B in flood damages
- Navigation: 652 miles of commercially navigable waterway
- Water supply: drinking water for 5 million people
- Recreation: 11,000 miles of shoreline

KEY INVESTMENTS 2025
- Grid Modernization: $1.2B invested in transmission upgrades
- Renewable Integration: 1,400 MW of new solar capacity added
- Grid Storage: 300 MW battery storage projects commissioned
- Cybersecurity: Enhanced NERC CIP compliance controls across all facilities

LOOKING AHEAD 2026
TVA targets 50% clean energy by 2030. Key initiatives:
- Small Modular Reactor (SMR) feasibility study at Clinch River site
- 2,000 MW additional solar capacity planned
- Advanced grid analytics deployment across all substations

TVA Regulatory Variance Request Process

PURPOSE
A regulatory variance request allows TVA business units to request temporary relief from an internal
policy, standard, or procedure when full compliance is not immediately achievable and compensating
controls can be established.

WHEN TO USE THIS PROCESS
- A new regulatory requirement cannot be implemented within the standard timeframe
- A system or process change creates a temporary compliance gap
- Technical constraints prevent full compliance and alternatives must be documented
- Emergency situations requiring immediate deviation from standard procedures

SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
All variance requests must include:
1. Requester name, title, department, and contact information
2. Specific policy/standard/requirement being varied
3. Technical justification explaining why full compliance is not possible
4. Risk assessment identifying potential impacts of the variance
5. Proposed compensating controls to mitigate identified risks
6. Implementation timeline with milestones for achieving full compliance
7. VP-level or above approval signature

SUBMISSION DEADLINES
- Standard variance: minimum 60 calendar days before required compliance date
- Emergency variance: minimum 5 business days with VP-level approval and documented urgency
- Post-incident variance: within 10 business days of discovering non-compliance

APPROVAL PROCESS
1. Submit completed request to regulatory.affairs@tva.gov
2. Regulatory Affairs reviews completeness (5 business days)
3. Legal and Compliance review (10 business days)
4. Risk Management assessment (5 business days)
5. Executive approval (VP or above) required for all variances
6. Written approval or denial issued within 30 calendar days of submission

APPROVED VARIANCE REQUIREMENTS
Approved variances must:
- Be tracked in TVA's Corrective Action Program (CAP)
- Include quarterly progress updates to Regulatory Affairs
- Be re-evaluated if compensating controls become ineffective
- Be closed out formally upon achieving full compliance

ESCALATION
If a variance is denied and the requestor believes the denial is in error:
